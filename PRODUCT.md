# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary Users:**
- **Shoppers**: Local customers in Mamburao, Occidental Mindoro looking to visualize furniture in their homes before purchase. Using mobile and desktop browsers. Looking for trustworthy, convenient furniture shopping with visual confidence.
- **Store Owners**: Local furniture retailers in the region managing inventory and reaching customers. Using desktop for dashboard management, mobile for occasional checks.

**Context**: Filipino local market, non-urban area. Users value trust, local craftsmanship pride, and practical visual tools. Not tech-native; needs simple, clear interfaces.

## Product Purpose

FurnishAR is a web-based AR and spatial visualization platform that bridges the gap between browsing furniture and purchasing with confidence. It lets shoppers overlay 3D furniture models in their real rooms via browser camera, scan room dimensions without measuring tools, and explore a digital catalog of local furniture inventory. Store owners get a management portal to maintain inventory. 

Success: Shoppers buy with confidence; store owners can compete with online retailers; both experience trusted, local commerce.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, practical, locally proud.** Like IKEA's approachability but rooted in Filipino craftsmanship. Clean and trustworthy—not corporate coldness, not minimalist detachment. The tone is helpful and encouraging, never patronizing.

**3 words**: Warm, accessible, trustworthy

## Anti-references

- **What NOT to do**: Corporate fintech coldness (navy + gold sterility), AI startup slickness, minimalist tech pretense, overly complex dashboards, cluttered e-commerce sites with too many competing elements, harsh dark modes for a local furniture store
- Avoid: Gradient text, glassmorphism, bounce animations, hero-metric templates, identical card grids, redundant copy

## Design Principles

1. **Spatial clarity first**: AR works because you see furniture in your actual room. Every design choice serves that core job—don't hide it behind interactions.
2. **Local pride, not exoticism**: Design reflects Filipino craftsmanship and market realities. Warm tones, rounded forms, accessible language. Never patronizing.
3. **Reduce friction to purchase**: Every screen removes one barrier (scanning, searching, comparing, paying). Favor direct action over decoration.
4. **Trust through transparency**: Show dimensions, materials, real product photos, store info. No AI-generated images or false promises.
5. **Mobile-first, desktop-capable**: Most shoppers browse on phone. Desktop works for store owners managing inventory. Both surfaces equally considered, never as afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA minimum** across all screens
- **Touch targets**: Minimum 44x44px on mobile for all interactive elements
- **Reduced motion**: Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; slide transitions become direct cuts
- **Color alone cannot convey information**: Status indicators use shape + color
- **Keyboard navigation**: Full support for desktop users
- **Text at 120%+**: Layout must not break when text size increases
- **AR scanning**: Provide text alternatives and clear on-screen instructions for every gesture
